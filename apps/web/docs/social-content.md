## LinkedIn (5 posts)

**1. Problem-solution**
Sweden's forests are under pressure. Bark beetle outbreaks have accelerated since the 2005 storm season, and climate change is extending the flight season further each year.

Forest owners are still largely reactive — they find out about infestations when they visit or when a neighbour notices.

BeetleSense changes that with satellite-based early warning, AI risk scoring, and parcel-level monitoring — tools that were previously only available to large industrial forestry operations, now accessible to every private skogsägare.

If you work in forestry, forest finance, or land management in Sweden, I'd love to show you what we've built.

---

**2. EFI / institutional angle**
Forest intelligence is becoming a regulated space. The EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR), biodiversity monitoring requirements, and carbon crediting frameworks all require verifiable, parcel-level data.

BeetleSense is built for this moment. Our architecture aligns with the EFI ForestWard Observatory framework — the same Copernicus/Sentinel data stack used by European research institutions.

For forest managers and sustainability professionals: the tools for compliance and early warning are ready. Happy to demo.

---

**3. Drone pilot opportunity**
Sweden needs more certified drone operators doing forest surveys.

The combination of satellite early warning (which flags at-risk areas) and drone-based ground verification is becoming the standard for bark beetle response. But the workflow is fragmented — pilots use email, PDFs, and manual invoicing.

BeetleSense has a pilot platform: job board, mission management, automated invoicing. If you know certified drone operators looking for structured survey work, send them our way.

---

**4. Carbon/finance angle**
Swedish forests collectively store billions of tonnes of CO₂. Individual forest owners are increasingly asked to quantify, report, and even sell their carbon assets — but they have no tools to do it.

BeetleSense includes a carbon calculator that tracks stored carbon per parcel over time. The output is useful for estate planning, insurance valuation, and increasingly for voluntary carbon credit schemes.

If you work in forest finance, green lending, or sustainability reporting: worth a conversation.

---

**5. Team/build story**
We built BeetleSense because we couldn't find a tool that treated Swedish forest owners as real users — not as targets for expensive enterprise contracts or generic mapping tools.

The result: parcel-level beetle monitoring, AI-powered advice in Swedish and English, satellite integration, and a workflow for drone pilots — all in one platform.

We're in early access. If you're in Swedish forestry and want to try it, the demo is live at beetlesense.ai.
