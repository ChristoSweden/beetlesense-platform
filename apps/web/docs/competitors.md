# BeetleSense — Competitor Analysis

## Top 5 Competitors

### 1. Treemetrics
- **Core feature**: LiDAR-based forest inventory and timber volume estimation
- **Pricing**: Enterprise contracts, ~€5,000–€20,000/year depending on hectares
- **Weakness**: No bark beetle detection, no real-time satellite alerts, no AI companion — inventory-only, no active pest monitoring

### 2. Silvintelligence (Silva Terra)
- **Core feature**: Forest management planning software with regulatory compliance tools
- **Pricing**: ~€1,200–€3,500/year per forest manager
- **Weakness**: Desktop-first, poor mobile UX, no remote sensing integration, no AI analysis — essentially a digital forms tool

### 3. Planet Labs / Planet Forest
- **Core feature**: High-cadence satellite imagery subscription for forest change detection
- **Pricing**: Enterprise only, €50,000+/year
- **Weakness**: Raw data only — no interpretation layer, no beetle-specific models, requires GIS expertise, inaccessible to small/medium forest owners

### 4. Förena (Skogssällskapet digital tools)
- **Core feature**: Swedish forest management platform with advisory services
- **Pricing**: Bundled with Skogssällskapet membership (~€800–€2,500/year)
- **Weakness**: No AI, no satellite integration, advisory is human-only and slow (days to respond), no bark beetle early warning

### 5. GlobalForestWatch (WRI)
- **Core feature**: Free global deforestation and fire alert platform using Landsat/MODIS
- **Pricing**: Free (donor-funded)
- **Weakness**: Country/region level only — no parcel-level intelligence, no bark beetle specificity, no actionable management tools, no Swedish property registry integration

---

## 2 Underserved Gaps BeetleSense Can Own

### Gap 1: Parcel-level bark beetle early warning for private Swedish forest owners
No competitor combines property registry lookup (fastighets-ID), Sentinel-2 satellite monitoring, ground survey workflows, and AI-powered beetle risk scoring at the individual parcel level. This is BeetleSense's core defensible position.

### Gap 2: EFI ForestWard Observatory alignment — credentialed data contribution
The EFI ForestWard Observatory Grant (G-01-2026) creates a unique opportunity: BeetleSense can position itself as the citizen science + professional data collection layer for European forest biodiversity monitoring. No competitor is pursuing this institutional angle. Being an official EFI data partner creates trust, regulatory credibility, and a moat that money alone cannot buy.

## Strategic Note
The EFI Grant (G-01-2026) strongly validates BeetleSense's technical approach. Copernicus/Sentinel data integration, biodiversity assessment, fire risk modelling, and carbon tracking are already built or in progress. The grant alignment is not just a revenue opportunity — it is a credentialing event that separates BeetleSense from all five competitors above.
