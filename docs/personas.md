# BeetleSense.ai — User Personas

## 1. Erik Lindqvist — Forest Owner

**Age**: 58
**Location**: Småland, Sweden
**Forest holdings**: ~320 hectares of mixed coniferous forest (spruce-dominant), inherited from his father
**Education**: Agricultural school; no formal IT training
**Role in platform**: Forest Owner (primary customer)

### Goal
Keep his forest healthy and profitable. He wants to know the moment something goes wrong — beetles, storm damage, disease — and he wants someone to tell him in plain Swedish what to do about it. He also wants accurate timber volume estimates before negotiating with buyers.

### Biggest Frustration
He found beetle damage on 14 hectares last summer — three months after it started. The infestation had already spread to adjacent stands by the time Skogsstyrelsen contacted him. He lost an estimated 180,000 SEK in timber value. He felt blindsided and angry. No one warned him. He checked the Skogsstyrelsen website but the data was months old and hard to interpret.

### Skill Level
- **Technical**: Low. Uses a smartphone (Samsung Galaxy) daily for calls, photos, weather, and Swish payments. Has never used GIS software. Can browse a website and fill in forms, but gets frustrated by complex navigation or small text. Types slowly.
- **Forestry**: High. Decades of hands-on experience. Knows his stands intimately. Can identify tree species and common damage types by sight, but lacks formal training in inventory methods or regulatory reporting.
- **English**: Basic. Prefers everything in Swedish.

### Primary Device
- **In the field**: Samsung Galaxy A-series phone (mid-range Android), often with poor 4G coverage
- **At home**: Laptop (Windows), used for email and banking. Never installed an app on a computer.

### Emotional Needs
- **Trust**: He needs to believe the platform actually knows what it is talking about. If it gives him a confidence score, he wants to understand what that means. If it cites research, good — that builds trust.
- **Control**: He does not want to feel dependent on technology he does not understand. He wants to feel like the platform works for him, not the other way around.
- **Reassurance**: After the beetle loss, he is anxious every spring. He wants proactive alerts — "Your parcels look healthy this week" is almost as valuable as a damage warning.
- **Simplicity**: If it takes more than 3 taps to do something on his phone in the forest, he will give up and call his neighbour instead.
- **Respect**: He has 40 years of forest knowledge. The AI Companion should feel like a knowledgeable colleague, not a teacher lecturing a student.

### How He Discovers BeetleSense
Through his local LRF (Lantbrukarnas Riksförbund) branch, which hosts a presentation about digital forest tools. He signs up on his phone during the coffee break.

## 2. Anna Bergström — Certified Drone Pilot

**Age**: 34
**Location**: Based in Jönköping; operates across southern Sweden
**Business**: Solo drone operator (AB company), certified A2/STS with 3 years of forestry survey experience
**Equipment**: DJI Matrice 350 RTK + P1 camera; DJI Mavic 3 Multispectral; planning to add BeetleSense e-nose payload
**Role in platform**: Drone Pilot (service provider in the pilot network)

### Goal
Build a sustainable business by flying more missions with less downtime between them. She wants a steady flow of jobs from forest owners, clear capture guidelines so she does not have to re-fly, and fast payment. She also wants her professional reputation to grow through verified, high-quality deliveries.

### Biggest Frustration
Her current workflow is fragmented: clients contact her by phone or email, she flies, processes orthomosaics in Pix4D or WebODM, uploads to Dropbox, sends an invoice manually, and then spends hours on the phone explaining results to non-technical forest owners. She loses about 30% of her billable time on administration. When a client asks her to re-fly because "the photos are blurry," she often cannot tell if it was the capture conditions or the client's expectations.

### Skill Level
- **Technical**: High. Comfortable with GIS software (QGIS, Google Earth Pro), photogrammetry pipelines, file formats (GeoTIFF, LAS, shapefile), coordinate systems. Manages her own website. Can troubleshoot software issues.
- **Forestry**: Moderate. Understands basic forest inventory concepts from working with clients. Not a trained forester. Relies on the platform's AI modules for the analysis layer.
- **English**: Fluent. Comfortable in either Swedish or English interfaces.

### Primary Device
- **Primary work device**: MacBook Pro 16" (processes orthomosaics, reviews jobs, manages uploads)
- **In the field**: iPad Pro (flight planning with DJI Pilot 2) + iPhone 15 Pro (comms, quick uploads)
- **Flight controller**: DJI RC Plus

### Emotional Needs
- **Professional recognition**: She wants to be seen as a skilled professional, not a "drone hobbyist." A public profile with verified credentials, ratings, and completed job count matters to her.
- **Efficiency**: Time is money. If the upload process is slow or requires converting file formats, she will be frustrated. She wants drag-and-drop upload of her standard output files.
- **Predictability**: She needs to know upfront what a job pays, what the capture requirements are (altitude, overlap, GSD), and when she will get paid. Ambiguity kills her scheduling.
- **Growth**: She is investing in her business. She wants to see an earnings dashboard, track her month-over-month revenue, and understand which modules are most in demand so she can market herself accordingly.
- **Fair treatment**: If a job gets cancelled after she has already driven to the site, she expects compensation. If upload fails and she has to re-upload, she expects it not to count against her.

### How She Discovers BeetleSense
She sees a post in the Swedish UAS Operators Facebook group about a platform that connects pilots with forest owners. She signs up the same day.

## 3. Magnus Eklund — Forest Inspector

**Age**: 47
**Location**: Gothenburg, Sweden
**Employer**: Mid-sized forest valuation firm (12 employees) serving insurance companies, banks, and estate settlements
**Certifications**: Registered forest inspector (Skogsstyrelsen), certified property valuator
**Role in platform**: Forest Inspector (specialist user)

### Goal
Produce accurate, legally defensible forest valuations faster. He needs standardised data that holds up in court, in insurance claims, and in bank lending decisions. His valuations are scrutinised by lawyers, auditors, and sometimes judges. Every number needs a source. He also wants to reduce the number of physical site visits required per valuation.

### Biggest Frustration
He spends 60% of his time on data collection and formatting, and only 40% on actual analysis and valuation. He currently gathers data from Skogsstyrelsen's web tools, orders separate drone surveys, manually enters numbers into Excel templates, cross-references against published KNN data, and assembles reports in Word. The data never lines up cleanly — different coordinate systems, different dates, different formats. He has been burned twice by using outdated satellite imagery that did not reflect recent storm damage.

### Skill Level
- **Technical**: Moderate-high. Expert in Excel, comfortable with PDF tools, has used Skogsstyrelsen's online map viewer extensively. Has basic QGIS experience but prefers pre-processed data. Does not code.
- **Forestry**: Expert. University-trained forester (SLU) with 20+ years of field experience. Deep knowledge of Swedish forest law, valuation standards (Skogsencyklopedin), and Skogsstyrelsen guidelines.
- **English**: Good. Reads English research papers. Prefers Swedish for day-to-day work.

### Primary Device
- **Primary**: Desktop PC (Windows 11) with dual 27" monitors at the office. This is where 80% of his work happens.
- **In the field**: Laptop (Lenovo ThinkPad) + smartphone for photos. Occasionally uses a tablet for on-site client meetings.
- **Printing**: Produces physical reports regularly. PDF formatting and print layout matter.

### Emotional Needs
- **Credibility**: His professional reputation depends on the accuracy and defensibility of his reports. If BeetleSense data contains errors, it is his name on the valuation. He needs audit trails, source citations, methodology descriptions, and confidence intervals.
- **Authority**: He is the expert in the room. The platform should give him better data faster — not try to replace his judgement. The AI Companion should present options and evidence, not make decisions for him.
- **Completeness**: A report missing one data layer is useless to him. He needs all relevant open data (KNN, LiDAR, property boundaries, damage history) pre-assembled for each parcel. Gaps are unacceptable.
- **Time savings**: He bills by the hour, but his firm wants him to handle more cases per month. If BeetleSense saves him 4 hours per valuation, that is the selling point — not flashy features.
- **Standards compliance**: His reports must align with Skogsstyrelsen valuation guidelines. Non-standard formats or missing fields mean he has to redo the work manually.

### How He Discovers BeetleSense
His firm's managing director sees a demo at a forestry conference (Elmia Wood) and asks Magnus to evaluate the platform for the team.
